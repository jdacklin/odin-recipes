Kung Pao Chicken

Description

Kung Pao Chicken is a traditional chinese dish made with chicken, vegetables, peanuts, and chilli peppers. This easy to make stir-fry dish is sweet and savory with a hint of mild spice.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch, dissolved in 2 tablespoons water
  • 2 tablespoons white wine
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breast (cut into chunks)
  • 1 ounce hot chile paste
  • 2 teaspoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon distilled white vinegar
  • 1 (8 ounce) can water chestnuts
  • 4 ounces chopped peanuts
  • 4 chopped green onions
  • 1 tablespoon chopped garlic

Steps

  1. Step 1

    Combine water and cornstarch in a cup. Set aside.

  2. Step 2

    Combine 1 tablespoon wine, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 tablespoon sesame oil, and 1 tablespoon cornstarch/water in a large glass bowl. Add chicken pieces and toss to coat. Cover dih and refrigerate for about 30 minutes.

  3. Step 3

    Combine remaining 1 tablespoon wine, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 tablespoon sesame oil, and remaining cornstarch/water mixture in a medium bowl. Whisk in chile paste, brown sugar, and vinegar. Add water chestnuts, peanuts, green onions, and garlic and toss to coat.

  4. Step 4

    Transfer water chestnut mixture to a medium skillet. Heat slowly over medium heat until aromatic.

  5. Step 5

    Remove chicken from marinade. Add to a large skillet and cook over medium-high heat, stirring, until chicken is cooked through and juices run clear.
